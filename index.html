Question of the Day

Test your knowledge with a daily challenge.

A 55-year-old male presents with right upper quadrant pain. An ultrasound reveals a well-defined, hyperechoic mass in the liver. What is the most likely diagnosis?

Correct Answer: C) Hemangioma. Hemangiomas are the most common benign liver tumors and typically appear as well-defined, hyperechoic masses on ultrasound.

Spotter of the Day

Can you identify the finding?

What is the key finding in this right breast MLO view?

Diagnosis: Fibroadenolipoma (Hamartoma) of the Breast.

Finding: This view demonstrates a large, well-circumscribed mass containing both fat (radiolucent) and glandular tissue (radiopaque), creating a "breast within a breast" appearance, which is classic for a fibroadenolipoma.
